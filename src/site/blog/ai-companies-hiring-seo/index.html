Why AI Companies Need SEO Experts Now

There is an irony here worth examining. The companies building the AI systems that some believe will replace search engines are simultaneously investing heavily in search engine optimization. Why?

AI Products Still Compete in Google Results

Claude.ai, ChatGPT, and Perplexity are web applications. People discover them through Google searches like “best AI chatbot,” “AI writing tool,” or “ChatGPT alternative.” Without strong organic rankings, these products lose visibility to competitors. OpenAI's domains receive an estimated 76.5 million organic visits per month — that is not accidental. It is the result of deliberate, large-scale SEO investment.

Content Is the Growth Engine

AI companies produce enormous volumes of content: documentation, research papers, blog posts, product pages, help articles, and developer guides. Each piece of content is a potential organic search entry point. But only if it is properly optimized. Technical SEO issues — missing meta tags, broken canonical URLs, poor internal linking, absent schema markup — can make thousands of pages effectively invisible to search engines and AI crawlers alike.

The Paradox: Disrupting Search While Depending on It

This is the core paradox. Anthropic builds Claude, which answers questions directly (potentially reducing traditional search). Yet Anthropic needs Google traffic to acquire Claude users. OpenAI builds ChatGPT with web search, which competes with Google. Yet OpenAI depends on Google organic traffic for its own growth. Perplexity is literally an AI search engine — and it hires SEO specialists to rank in Google.

“The companies building AI search understand something that the 'SEO is dead' crowd does not: organic visibility is an asset. It compounds over time, it costs nothing per click, and it works across every search surface — traditional and AI-powered.”

The takeaway is straightforward: if the most well-funded AI companies in the world are investing hundreds of thousands of dollars in SEO talent, the discipline has real, measurable value.

The Skills They Are Looking For

What exactly do these $200K–$320K job postings require? We reviewed the listed qualifications across multiple AI company SEO roles and identified six recurring skill categories.

1. Traditional SEO (Still Foundational)

Every posting we reviewed lists core SEO skills: technical auditing, keyword research, on-page optimization, crawl management, site architecture, and link analysis. Traditional SEO is the foundation. It has not been replaced — it has been added to.

2. AEO: Answer Engine Optimization

Anthropic's job posting explicitly mentions optimizing for AI-powered search and answer engines. This means structuring content so it can be parsed, understood, and cited by systems like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. AEO involves TL;DR sections, clear definitions, FAQ markup, citation-ready formatting, and content that directly answers user questions. Read our full AEO guide for a deep dive.

3. GEO: Generative Engine Optimization

GEO focuses on visibility in AI-generated search results — Google AI Overviews, Bing Copilot, and similar surfaces. This includes topical authority, comprehensive content coverage, entity recognition, knowledge graph alignment, and snippet-optimized content structure. See our complete GEO guide for more detail.

4. Programmatic SEO at Scale

AI companies operate at scale. OpenAI has thousands of indexable pages across its domains. Anthropic's documentation alone spans hundreds of pages. The SEO leads they hire need to think programmatically: templated page optimization, automated schema generation, bulk meta tag management, and scalable content systems. This is not about manually tweaking individual pages.

5. Content Strategy for the AI Era

Content at AI companies serves multiple purposes: organic acquisition, user education, developer documentation, and brand positioning. The SEO lead must align content strategy with AI-era search behavior — anticipating how users phrase questions to AI assistants, what content structures get cited, and how to build topical authority across a complex product ecosystem.

6. Technical SEO + Engineering Mindset

AI companies are engineering-driven organizations. Their SEO leads need to work with developers, understand CMS architecture, write or review structured data implementations, debug crawl issues at scale, and make data-driven decisions using tools like Google Search Console, BigQuery, and custom analytics. This is not a marketing-only role — it is a technical discipline.

AEO and GEO Explained — Quick Primer

If you are unfamiliar with AEO and GEO, here is a brief overview of why these skills command premium salaries.

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

AEO is the practice of optimizing content for AI assistants that provide direct answers: ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google Gemini. These systems do not show ten blue links. They synthesize information from multiple sources and present a single answer. To be cited, your content needs clear structure, definitive statements, FAQ sections, and citation-ready formatting.

Key AEO signals include:

  • TL;DR or summary sections at the top of content
  • Clear, concise definitions that can be extracted as answers
  • FAQ sections with structured data (FAQPage schema)
  • Authoritative sourcing with named authors and credentials
  • Content organized in question-and-answer format

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

GEO focuses on visibility within AI-generated search summaries like Google AI Overviews and Bing Copilot responses. These AI systems select content based on topical authority, content comprehensiveness, entity relationships, and structural signals.

Key GEO signals include:

  • Topical authority (depth and breadth of coverage on a subject)
  • Knowledge graph alignment (entities, relationships, structured data)
  • Comprehensive content with statistics, examples, and expert analysis
  • Image captions, alt text, and visual content supporting text claims
  • Internal linking that demonstrates topical clusters

Together, AEO and GEO extend traditional SEO into AI-powered search surfaces. They do not replace SEO — they build on it. For a detailed comparison, see our SEO vs AEO vs GEO comparison.
