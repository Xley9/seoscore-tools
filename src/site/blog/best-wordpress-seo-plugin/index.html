Why This Comparison Is Different (We Test AEO & GEO Too)

Every WordPress SEO plugin comparison published in 2025 and 2026 follows the same formula: compare meta tag editing, XML sitemaps, schema options, keyword tracking, and pricing. That was sufficient when Google's ten blue links were the only game in town. It is not sufficient anymore.

In 2026, a significant and growing share of search traffic originates from AI-powered systems:

  • Google AI Overviews now appear for over 40% of search queries, pulling content from pages that are structured for machine readability.

This means your SEO plugin needs to do more than optimize for Google's crawler. It needs to optimize for AI readers too. That is where AEO and GEO come in — and that is exactly the dimension that Yoast and RankMath do not test.

This small addition — testing AEO and GEO alongside traditional SEO — changes the entire ranking of which plugin is "best." A plugin that scores 80/100 on SEO but 0/100 on AEO and GEO is a plugin that ignores two-thirds of the modern search landscape. And you cannot fix what you cannot measure.

What are AEO and GEO?

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) optimizes content for AI assistants and answer engines — think FAQ schema, Speakable markup, structured Q&A, and direct-answer formatting. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) optimizes content to be cited by generative AI systems — think entity markup, citation-ready summaries, unique data, expert signals, and source credibility markers. Yoast SEO Premium: What You Get for $118/Year

Yoast SEO is the most recognized WordPress SEO plugin, with over 12 million active installations. It was one of the first plugins to bring on-page SEO analysis to WordPress, and its green/orange/red traffic light system became synonymous with WordPress SEO. But recognition is not the same as capability — especially in 2026.

Strengths

  • Brand recognition: The most trusted name in WordPress SEO. Extensive documentation, large community, and reliable support.
  • Readability analysis: Flesch Reading Ease scoring, sentence length checks, paragraph length suggestions, passive voice detection.
  • Redirect manager (Premium): Automatic 301 redirects when you change a slug. Useful for content migrations.
  • Internal linking suggestions (Premium): Recommends related posts to link to while writing.
  • Social previews: Visual preview of how your page will look when shared on Facebook and Twitter.

Weaknesses

  • No AEO checks at all. Yoast does not analyze FAQ schema quality, Speakable markup, answer formatting, or voice search readiness. Zero AEO coverage.
  • No GEO checks at all. No analysis of citation readiness, entity markup, generative AI compatibility, or source authority signals. Zero GEO coverage.
  • No auto-fix capability. Yoast identifies issues but requires you to fix every single one manually. No one-click fix for meta tags, schema, security headers, or Open Graph.
  • No AI content generation. No Claude or GPT integration for generating FAQ sections, summaries, expert content, or comparison tables.
  • Subscription model: $118/year, every year, forever. Stop paying and you lose Premium features including the redirect manager.
  • Limited check count: Approximately 50–60 checks focused exclusively on traditional SEO factors.

Rank Math Pro: What You Get for $96/Year

Rank Math burst onto the scene as the "everything free" alternative to Yoast. Its free version is genuinely impressive — offering features that Yoast locks behind its premium tier. Rank Math Pro adds keyword tracking, Google Analytics integration, and advanced schema templates. But like Yoast, it was built for a search world that no longer exists in isolation.

Strengths

  • Generous free tier: The free version includes XML sitemaps, Open Graph, basic schema, redirections, and 404 monitoring — features Yoast charges for.
  • Schema template system: Custom schema templates with variables. More flexible than Yoast's schema implementation.
  • Keyword rank tracking (Pro): Built-in keyword position monitoring without a separate tool.
  • Google Analytics integration (Pro): View traffic data directly in the WordPress dashboard.
  • Multiple keyword focus: Optimize for up to 5 focus keywords per post (unlimited in Pro).

Weaknesses

  • No AEO checks. Like Yoast, Rank Math does not analyze answer engine readiness, FAQ schema quality, Speakable markup, or voice search optimization.
  • No GEO checks. No analysis of generative AI citation potential, entity disambiguation, or source credibility signals.
  • No auto-fix. Issues are flagged but must be resolved manually. No one-click batch fix for technical SEO problems.
  • No AI content generation. No integration with language models for content enhancement or FAQ generation.
  • Subscription pricing: $96/year for a single personal site. $192/year for the Business plan (100 sites). Stop paying, lose Pro features.
  • Check count: Approximately 60–80 checks, all focused on traditional SEO factors.
