Quick Answer

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing web content to be cited by AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Unlike traditional SEO, which targets Google's blue-link search results, AEO targets the citations and sources that AI engines reference when generating synthesized answers. The 8 core AEO signals are AI-crawler robots.txt access, llms.txt presence, FAQPage schema, Speakable Specification, direct-answer formatting, author E-E-A-T credentials, citation-ready facts, and content extractability.

What is AEO?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. The term gained traction in 2023-2024 as ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity began driving meaningful referral traffic to websites that had positioned themselves as citation sources. By 2026, AEO is a distinct discipline alongside (not replacing) traditional SEO.

The core insight: AI answer engines do not show users a list of clickable links. They synthesize an answer and cite their sources inline. Whether your site gets cited — and how prominently — depends on a different set of signals than what Google's classic ranking algorithm evaluates.

An AI answer engine deciding which sources to cite for a query typically goes through this sequence: (1) retrieve candidate URLs via search index or crawl, (2) extract structured content from each candidate, (3) evaluate trustworthiness signals, (4) prefer sources with extractable, citation-friendly content patterns, (5) attribute citations in the final answer. AEO optimizes for every step in this pipeline.

Three forces have made AEO consequential:

  1. AI-mediated search is growing fast. ChatGPT alone hits ~700M weekly active users in mid-2026, with Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and Copilot adding hundreds of millions more. The 8 core AEO signals

Signal 1: AI-crawler access in robots.txt

AI engines use their own crawlers to discover and index content. Your robots.txt must explicitly allow them. Common offenders to allow: GPTBot, ChatGPT-User, ClaudeBot, Claude-Web, anthropic-ai, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended, Amazonbot. A site that blocks (or fails to explicitly allow) these crawlers will not appear in AI citations regardless of how good the content is.

Signal 2: llms.txt file

llms.txt is the AI-era equivalent of robots.txt. A file at yoursite.com/llms.txt that contains a citation-friendly summary of your site. Per the proposed spec: H1 with site name, blockquote with one-sentence description, sectioned content with citation-ready facts, source URLs, and key features. AI models that find a well-structured llms.txt cite the site more accurately and more frequently than equivalent sites without one.

See: LLMS.txt Setup Guide

Signal 3: FAQPage structured data

JSON-LD FAQPage schema with explicit Question and Answer types. AI engines prefer to extract Q&A pairs as discrete citation units. FAQPage markup makes this extraction trivial. Even pages that are not literally FAQs benefit from adding 3-5 Q&A blocks with FAQPage schema at the bottom.

Signal 4: Speakable Specification

JSON-LD SpeakableSpecification with cssSelector or xpath pointing at the parts of your page meant for voice/AI extraction. Originally designed for voice assistants, now used by AI engines as a hint about which content blocks the author considers most citation-worthy.

Signal 5: Direct-answer formatting

The first 100-200 words of any article should directly answer the implicit question in the headline. Quick Answer / TL;DR / Key Takeaways blocks at the top of articles dramatically increase the probability that AI engines extract your content. The pattern: H1 → 1-paragraph direct answer → expanded body content.

Signal 6: Author E-E-A-T credentials

Author byline with name, role, and qualifications. Person schema with sameAs links to verifiable profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub, ORCID, Wikipedia). Organization schema with foundingDate, address, contact. AI engines weight credibility heavily because hallucinated or low-quality citations damage the engine's reputation. Anonymous content is rarely cited.

Signal 7: Citation-ready facts

Short, attributable statistical claims with explicit source attribution. Example: “ChatGPT had 700 million weekly active users as of May 2026 (source: OpenAI press release, May 2026)” beats “ChatGPT is hugely popular” because the former is directly quotable.

Signal 8: Content extractability

Short paragraphs (20-80 words). Clear definition sentences. Numbered lists where helpful. Tables for comparisons. Code blocks for technical content. Anything that lets the AI engine grab a clean, self-contained chunk to cite without ambiguity.

The three disciplines share a ~60% signal overlap. Optimizing for AEO automatically improves SEO and GEO scores for most checks. The differentiation is in the remaining ~40%: AEO emphasizes crawler access + llms.txt + citation patterns; GEO emphasizes content depth + multi-perspective writing + author credentials.

How to do AEO in 2026 — practical checklist

  1. Audit your robots.txt. Explicitly Allow GPTBot, ChatGPT-User, ClaudeBot, Claude-Web, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended.
  2. Deploy llms.txt. See our guide. 200-400 lines covering site purpose, key facts, source URLs.
  3. Add FAQPage schema to your top 10 pages. 3-5 Q&A pairs each, with proper Question/Answer types.
  4. Add Speakable spec to your homepage and top articles, pointing at the lede paragraph and Quick Answer blocks.
  5. Restructure articles with Quick Answer blocks. First paragraph = direct answer in 50-80 words.
  6. Set up Person + Organization schema with sameAs links to your LinkedIn, GitHub, and any verifiable profile.
  7. Convert vague claims into citable facts. Add source URLs and dates.
  8. Run an AEO scan. Use seoscore.tools to surface the remaining ~40 checks. Fix Critical and High issues first.
  9. Measure month-over-month. Track AEO score via monthly snapshots, AI-referral sessions via Google Analytics.
  10. Iterate on cluster gaps. Combine AEO with topical authority — AI engines cite domains with comprehensive topic coverage 5-10x more than single-article hits.
