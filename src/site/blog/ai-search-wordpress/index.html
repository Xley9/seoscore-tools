What Is AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of optimizing your content to be selected as the direct answer in search features like Featured Snippets, People Also Ask boxes, voice search results, and AI-generated answers. While traditional SEO focuses on ranking in a list of links, AEO WordPress optimization focuses on being the answer — the single source chosen to directly answer a user's question.

Where AEO Matters

  • Google Featured Snippets: The "position zero" answer box that appears above organic results. Featured snippets are sourced from pages with clear, structured answers.
  • People Also Ask (PAA): The expandable question boxes in Google results. Pages with FAQ schema and clear question-answer formats dominate PAA results.
  • Voice Search: Smart speakers and voice assistants read one answer per query. That answer comes from content with speakable markup and concise, paragraph-format answers.
  • Google AI Overviews: The AI-generated summaries use AEO-optimized content as primary source material for synthesizing answers.
  • ChatGPT & Perplexity: AI search engines preferentially cite content that provides clear, attributable answers to specific questions.

Key AEO Factors for WordPress

  • FAQ schema (FAQPage) — Wraps question-answer pairs in structured data that search engines and AI can directly consume
  • Speakable markup — Identifies content sections suitable for voice assistant readout using schema.org/speakable
  • Clear definitions — First-paragraph answers in "X is Y" format that match answer engine extraction patterns
  • Question-based H2/H3 headings — Content structured around the actual questions users ask
  • Concise paragraph answers — 40-60 word answers immediately following question headings for snippet extraction
  • HowTo schema — Step-by-step content marked up for rich results and voice assistant instructions

What Is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your content to be cited and referenced by AI-powered search engines. While AEO focuses on being the answer, GEO focuses on being the cited source — the page that ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, and Google AI Overviews link back to when they generate responses.

How AI Search Engines Choose Sources

AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity do not rank pages the same way Google does. Instead, they evaluate content for citability: can this source be confidently attributed? Is the entity behind it clearly defined? Does the structured data confirm the content's authority? Here are the key GEO ranking factors:

  • Authority signals — Organization schema, author markup, E-E-A-T indicators that establish the source as trustworthy
  • Structured data depth — Rich schema markup (Product, Article, FAQ, HowTo, Organization) that gives AI parseable entity information
  • Entity clarity — Clear definitions of what your business/product/content is, using consistent naming and schema
  • Content attribution readiness — Paragraphs structured for direct quoting, with clear topic sentences and attributable claims
  • llms.txt — A machine-readable file (like robots.txt for AI) that tells language models what your site is about and how to cite it
  • Semantic HTML structure — Proper heading hierarchy, <article> tags, <time> elements, and semantic markup that AI models can reliably parse
  • Freshness signals — dateModified in schema, recent timestamps, and content that signals current relevance

Default WordPress themes and standard plugins were built for a world of ten blue links, not a world of AI-generated answers. Here is what a typical WordPress site is missing.

What Default WordPress Lacks

  • No FAQ schema: WordPress has no built-in way to create FAQ sections with FAQPage structured data. Without it, your content is invisible to Featured Snippets and AI answer extraction.
  • No speakable markup: Zero voice search optimization out of the box. Smart speakers and voice assistants cannot identify which parts of your content to read aloud.
  • No llms.txt: WordPress does not generate an llms.txt file. AI search engines have no machine-readable guide to your site's purpose and citation preferences.
  • No Organization schema: No structured business identity data. AI engines cannot confidently attribute content to your organization.
  • No entity clarity: Default WordPress themes produce generic HTML with no semantic entity markup. AI models struggle to understand what your site is about.
  • No AI citation readiness: Content is formatted for human readers, not for AI extraction and citation. Paragraphs lack the structure that AI engines need for confident attribution.
