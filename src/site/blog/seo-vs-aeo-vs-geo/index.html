SEO, AEO, and GEO are the three pillars of modern search optimization. SEO (Search Engine Optimization) targets traditional search rankings. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) targets AI assistants like ChatGPT and Perplexity. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) targets AI-powered search results like Google AI Overview. Together, they ensure your website is discoverable across every platform where people search for information in 2026.

If you're only optimizing for traditional SEO, you're missing two rapidly growing channels where your audience is looking for information. This guide breaks down all three disciplines, shows you exactly how they differ, explains where they overlap, and helps you decide which to prioritize based on your situation.

What is SEO? (Search Engine Optimization)

SEO is the practice of optimizing your website to rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs). It's the oldest and most established form of search optimization, dating back to the mid-1990s. According to Google's SEO Starter Guide, SEO encompasses everything from technical website health to content quality to link building.

Key SEO ranking factors include:

  • Technical SEO: Page speed, mobile responsiveness, Core Web Vitals, crawlability, HTTPS, proper URL structure
  • On-page SEO: Title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure, keyword optimization, internal linking, image alt text
  • Content quality: Comprehensive coverage, originality, freshness, user intent alignment
  • Off-page SEO: Backlinks from authoritative domains, brand mentions, social signals
  • User experience: Low bounce rate, high dwell time, engagement metrics

SEO is the foundation that AEO and GEO build upon. Without solid SEO, your content won't be indexed, won't be crawlable, and won't have the technical infrastructure that AI systems rely on.

What is AEO? (Answer Engine Optimization)

AEO is the practice of optimizing your content so AI-powered assistants like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini can find, understand, and cite your website. Unlike SEO which targets search result rankings, AEO targets the conversational AI tools that millions of users now use as their primary way to get answers.

Key AEO factors include:

  • Q&A content format: Explicit questions as headings with clear, concise answers below
  • FAQ sections: Dedicated FAQ blocks with FAQPage Schema.org markup
  • Structured data: Comprehensive Schema.org implementation (Article, HowTo, FAQPage, Speakable)
  • Definitive definitions: Clear, bolded, quotable definitions in the first paragraph
  • Content extractability: Well-organized content that AI can parse and cite accurately
  • Topical authority: Deep coverage of a topic across multiple interconnected pages

AEO is growing rapidly because AI assistant usage is exploding. ChatGPT alone has over 200 million weekly active users. When these users ask a question, they expect an answer — and AI systems cite the sources they trust most. Read our complete AEO guide →

What is GEO? (Generative Engine Optimization)

GEO is the practice of optimizing your content to be featured in AI-generated search results like Google AI Overview, Bing Copilot, and SearchGPT. These platforms generate comprehensive summaries above traditional search results, citing sources that contribute to the answer.

Key GEO factors include:

  • E-E-A-T signals: Demonstrated Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness
  • Citation-worthy content: Original data, statistics, expert analysis, unique insights
  • Content depth: Comprehensive coverage that serves as a definitive resource
  • Brand entity recognition: Consistent brand presence across the web
  • Authoritative sourcing: References to academic papers, official documentation, industry standards
  • Content diversity: Mix of paragraphs, lists, tables, definitions, and blockquotes

GEO is critical because AI-generated summaries are becoming the primary search interface. For many queries, the AI summary takes up the entire above-the-fold area, pushing traditional results below the fold. Read our complete GEO guide →

How SEO, AEO, and GEO Work Together

SEO, AEO, and GEO are not competing strategies — they're complementary layers that build on each other. Think of them as a three-layer optimization model:

Layer 1: SEO is the foundation. It ensures your content is technically sound, properly indexed, and ranks in search results. Without SEO, AI systems can't find your content in the first place.

Layer 2: AEO extends your reach to AI assistants. It structures your content so conversational AI tools can extract and cite your answers. AEO builds on SEO by adding structured data, Q&A format, and citation-ready content.

Layer 3: GEO captures AI-powered search. It optimizes for the AI-generated summaries that appear above traditional results. GEO builds on both SEO and AEO by adding authority signals, original data, and citation-worthy writing.

The overlap is significant. Research suggests that approximately 60-70% of optimization factors are shared across all three disciplines. Here's what's shared and what's unique:

Shared Across All Three

  • Quality content with clear structure and proper headings
  • Schema.org structured data
  • Mobile-responsive, fast-loading pages
  • Internal linking and logical site architecture
  • Fresh, regularly updated content

SEO-Specific

  • Backlink profile and domain authority
  • Core Web Vitals performance
  • Keyword research and optimization
  • SERP feature targeting (snippets, image packs)

AEO-Specific

  • FAQ sections with FAQPage schema
  • Speakable markup for voice-ready content
  • Conversational, question-answer format
  • Definitive definitions in opening paragraphs

GEO-Specific

  • E-E-A-T signals (credentials, experience, authority)
  • Original data and statistics with sources
  • Brand entity recognition across the web
  • Citation-worthy writing style

Which Should You Focus On?

The answer depends on where you are in your optimization journey. Here's a practical framework:

If you're just starting: Focus on SEO first

Before optimizing for AI, make sure your website has a solid technical foundation. Fix crawl errors, optimize page speed, implement proper meta tags, create quality content with logical heading structure, and build a clean site architecture. SEO is the prerequisite for everything else. A page that can't be found by Google also can't be found by AI systems that rely on Google's index.

If your SEO is solid (score 70+): Add AEO

Once your technical SEO and content quality are in good shape, start optimizing for AI assistants. Add FAQ sections to your key pages with proper FAQPage schema. Restructure content with clear Q&A headings. Add Speakable markup to your most important definitions and summaries. Implement comprehensive Schema.org markup across your site. These changes also improve your SEO, so you're building on your foundation without compromising it.

If you want full coverage: Layer on GEO

With solid SEO and AEO in place, add the GEO layer. Strengthen your E-E-A-T signals by adding author bios, credentials, and about pages. Include original data and statistics in your content. Write in a citation-worthy style with clear, authoritative statements. Build brand entity recognition through consistent presence across the web. This positions your content for maximum visibility across traditional search, AI assistants, and AI-powered search results simultaneously.
